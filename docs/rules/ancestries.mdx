# Ancestries

Your ancestry is your lineage, heritage, and how others see you at first glance. Choose 1 ancestry and add its bonus to your character sheet.

<Callout type="tip">
**Flavor Is Free.** Want to play a Stout Halfling instead of an Elusive one? An Optimistic Human instead of a Tenacious one? A Frogfolk instead of a Bunbun? As long as it makes sense and your GM agrees, go for it!
</Callout>

---

## Common Ancestries

### Human (Medium)

Found in every terrain and environment, humans' curiosity and ambition drive them to explore every corner of the world.

**Tenacious.** +1 to all skills and Initiative.

---

### Dwarf (Medium)

"Dwarf," in the old language, means "stone." Resilient, solid, and stout — even when driven to exhaustion, a Dwarf will not falter. Gifted with physical vitality and a belly that can handle the finest (and worst) consumables the world has to offer.

**Stout.** +2 max Hit Dice, +1 max Wounds, –1 Speed. You know Dwarvish if your INT is not negative.

---

### Elf (Medium)

Tall and slender, elves epitomize swiftness, grace, and wit. Formidable in both diplomacy and combat, they strike swiftly — often preventing the worst by acting first.

**Lithe.** Advantage on Initiative, +1 Speed. You know Elvish if your INT is not negative.

<Callout type="note">
**Half-Elves?** Mix ancestries however makes sense. Pick one ancestral bonus, or use both at half effectiveness/frequency.
</Callout>

---

### Halfling (Small)

Kind of like a human, but smaller — except for the feet. Halflings don't know exactly where their luck comes from, but they have it in spades.

**Elusive.** +1 to Stealth. If you fail a save, you can succeed instead, 1/Safe Rest.

---

### Gnome (Small)

Eccentric, curious, and perpetually optimistic — gnomes pursue their passions with scatterbrained enthusiasm. Known for their tinkering and spreading cheer.

**Optimistic.** Allow an ally within Reach 6 to reroll any single die; resets when healed to your max HP. –1 Speed. You know Dwarvish if your INT is not negative (though you call it Gnomish, of course).

---

### Bunbun (Small)

Agile and unpredictable, Bunbun use their powerful legs to leap great distances and catch foes off guard — striking from unexpected angles and repositioning swiftly in combat.

**Bunny Legs.** Before Interposing or after Defending (after damage), hop up to your Speed in any direction for free, 1/encounter.

---

### Dragonborn (Medium)

The soul of a dragon burns within you; the scales of your body are like forged steel. You are a kiln and your heritage the coals that stoke your flames.

**Draconic Heritage.** +1 Armor. When you attack: deal an additional LVL+KEY damage (ignoring armor) divided as you choose among any of your targets; recharges when you Safe Rest or gain a Wound. You know Draconic if your INT is not negative.

---

### Fiendkin (Medium)

Said to have been born from the union of mortal and fiend, or from a cursed bloodline, Fiendkin often find themselves outcasts — yet embody determination in the face of adversity.

**Flameborn.** 1 of your neutral saves is advantaged instead. You know Infernal if your INT is not negative.

---

### Goblin (Small)

Green, cunning, and perpetually vilified, Goblins thrive on the edge of chaos. For a Goblin, vanishing into the shadows is not just a skill — it's an identity.

**Skedaddle.** Can move 2 spaces for free after you become the target of an attack or negative effect (after damage, ignoring difficult terrain). You know Goblin if your INT is not negative.

---

### Kobold (Small)

Small, often maniacal, and dragon-obsessed. Kobolds thrive in the shadows, finding ingenious ways to survive despite their diminutive size. Underestimated by many, they prove that even the smallest can wield great power.

**Wily.** Force an enemy to reroll a non-critical attack against you, 1/encounter. +3 to Influence with friendly characters. Advantage on skill checks related to dragons. You know Draconic if your INT is not negative.

---

### Orc (Medium)

Just when you think you've bested a mighty Orc, you've merely succeeded in rousing their anger. While others cower before death, Orcs boldly defy its grasp.

**Relentless.** When you would drop to 0 HP, set your HP to LVL instead, 1/Safe Rest. +1 Might. You know Goblin if your INT is not negative (though you call it Orcish).

---

## Exotic Ancestries

These ancestries are rarer and may not be available in all settings — check with your GM before selecting one.

<Callout type="tip">
**Flavor Is Free.** A leaping Frogfolk instead of a Bunbun? A Flameborn Kobold? A winged Fairy instead of a Birdfolk? A Badgerfolk instead of a Stoatling? As long as it makes sense and your GM is game, go for it!
</Callout>

---

### Birdfolk (Small or Medium)

Birdfolk find sanctuary in the boundless expanse of the sky. The gift of flight, however, comes with hollow bones and commensurate frailty.

**Hollow Bones.** You have a fly Speed as long as you wear armor no heavier than Leather. Crits against you are <RuleRef id="vicious" label="Vicious" /> (the attacker rolls 1 additional die). Forced movement moves you twice as far.

---

### Celestial (Medium)

Descendants of divine beings, Celestials carry an aura of nobility and grace. Their innate connection to higher planes lets them resist misfortune.

**Highborn.** Your disadvantaged save is Neutral instead. You know Celestial if your INT is not negative.

---

### Changeling (Medium)

Hunted for their silver blood, Changelings are natural survivors who slip into new identities with ease. Those who shift too often can lose themselves — becoming little more than reflections of faces they wear.

**New Place, New Face.** +2 shifting skill points. You may take on the appearance of any ancestry. When you do, place your 2 shifting skill points into any 1 skill. 1/day.

---

### Crystalborn (Medium)

Formed from living crystal, the Crystalborn are beings of dazzling beauty and otherworldly toughness. Their translucent bodies refract light and sound, granting unique combat abilities.

**Reflective Aura.** When you <RuleRef id="defend" />, gain KEY armor and deal KEY damage back to the attacker. 1/encounter.

---

### Dryad/Shroomling (Small or Medium)

Tied to the natural world, Dryads and Shroomlings embody the balance between flora and fauna. Their unique physiology releases toxic spores when harmed.

**Danger Pollen/Spores.** Whenever an enemy causes you one or more Wounds, you excrete soporific spores: all adjacent enemies are <RuleRef id="dazed" />. You know Elvish if your INT is not negative.

---

### Half-Giant (Large)

Towering beings whose strength is as immovable as the mountains they call home. Their sheer size and resilience make them fearsome opponents.

**Strength of Stone.** Force an enemy to reroll a crit against you, 1/encounter. +2 Might. You know Dwarvish if your INT is not negative.

---

### Minotaur/Beastfolk (Medium)

Minotaur and other Beastfolk embody a primal connection to The Wild, combining strength with natural agility. Their powerful build lets them move swiftly or charge with unstoppable force.

**Charge.** When you move at least 4 spaces, you can push a creature in your path. Medium: 1 space; Small/Tiny: up to 2 spaces. 1/turn.

---

### Oozeling/Construct (Small or Medium)

What even is a "people" anyway? If you can squish yourself into a pair of pants and swing a sword, who's to say you can't be a people too?!

**Odd Constitution.** Increment your Hit Dice one step (d6→d8→d10→d12→d20); they always heal you for the maximum amount. Magical healing always heals you for the minimum amount.

---

### Planarbeing (Medium)

Not from this plane of existence — your soul is not as strongly tethered to it as others. With this vulnerability comes power: the ability to temporarily shift from one plane to another.

**Planeshift.** Whenever you <RuleRef id="defend" />, you can gain 1 Wound to temporarily phase out of the material plane and ignore the damage. –2 max Wounds.

---

### Ratfolk (Small)

Ratfolk are survivors, thriving in the shadows of society where others fear to tread. Agile, resourceful, and fiercely loyal to their own — with a knack for turning scraps into solutions.

**Scurry.** Gain +2 armor if you moved on your last turn.

---

### Stoatling (Small)

Small but far from weak. With fierce determination and warrior hearts, Stoatlings can take down foes many times their size, exploiting larger enemies' weaknesses.

**Small But Ferocious.** Whenever you make a single-target attack against a creature larger than you, roll 1 additional d6 for each size category it is larger. They do the same.

---

### Turtlefolk (Small or Medium)

Patient, sturdy, and slow to anger. Turtlefolk rely on their thick shells for protection — making them difficult to harm, at the cost of speed.

**Slow & Steady.** +4 Armor, –2 Speed.

---

### Wyrdling (Small)

Unpredictable and chaotic, Wyrdlings are the result of magic gone awry. Their bodies pulse with raw arcane energy, and their mere presence often disturbs the balance of magic around them.

**Chaotic Surge.** Whenever you or a willing ally within Reach 6 casts a tiered spell, you may allow them to roll on the Chaos Table. 1/encounter.
