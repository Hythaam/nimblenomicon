# Size, Movement & Range

## Size Categories

Size determines how much space a creature occupies on the battlefield and interacts with various abilities (grappling, forced movement, etc.).

| Size | Description | Space |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Tiny** | Can be carried in a pocket | Many fit in 1 space |
| **Small** | Can be carried in a backpack | 2 fit in 1 space |
| **Medium** | Average human size | 1 per space |
| **Large** | Roughly the size of a bear | 1 per 2×2 area |
| **Huge** | Roughly the size of a small house | 1 per 3×3 area |
| **Gargantuan** | As large as a castle keep | 1 per 4×4 area or greater |

---

## Speed & Movement

A character's Speed determines how far they can move on their turn. The default Speed is **6 spaces**.

Each space on a standard grid represents roughly **5 ft. (1 meter)**. A hero with Speed 6 can move up to **6 spaces** horizontally or diagonally.

### Breaking Up Movement

Movement can be **split across other actions**. A hero can use multiple Move actions on a single turn to move multiple times.

### Moving Through Spaces

- You can move through spaces occupied by **allies** freely.
- You can move through spaces occupied by **enemies**, but this counts as <RuleRef id="difficult-terrain" /> (half speed).
- You cannot **end** your movement in an occupied space.

### Difficult Terrain

In <RuleRef id="difficult-terrain" />, movement speed is halved. Examples include:
- Moving through an enemy's space
- Climbing (trees, cliffsides, certain buildings)
- Deep water, mud, rubble

<Callout type="tip">
**Abstract Movement Option:** Skip counting spaces — measure rough movement from pinky to thumb on the table. Adjust slightly for faster or slower characters.
</Callout>

## Range & Reach

Abilities, weapons, and spells specify a **Range** or **Reach** indicating how far away a target can be. If none is specified, default to **Reach 1** (adjacent).

- **Reach** applies to melee attacks — Reach 1 means adjacent.
- **Range** applies to ranged attacks and spells.

### Melee Disadvantage (Ranged in Melee)

If **any enemy is adjacent to you**, Ranged attacks are made with <RuleRef id="disadvantage" />. Reach attacks are **not** affected.

### Long Range Extension

You can extend any Ranged attack by **+2 Range** by adding **1 die of disadvantage** to the attack roll (maximum +6 Range total).

<Callout type="tip">
**Abstracted Distances:** Use **Close**, **Midrange**, and **Far** instead of counting spaces. A move from Midrange can traverse to Close or Far. Close = Reach/Range 4; Midrange = up to Range 6; beyond = Far.
</Callout>

---

## Falling Damage

When a character falls, they take bludgeoning damage based on distance:

**1d6 bludgeoning damage per 10 ft. (2 spaces) fallen.**

See <RuleRef id="falling" /> for the full rule reference.

---

## Forced Movement

When a character is forcibly moved (pushed, thrown, knocked back) but stopped by an obstacle:

- They take **1d6 bludgeoning damage** for every space the movement is shortened.
- If they hit **another creature**, both creatures **split** this damage.

<Callout type="note">
Forced movement interacts with size — a Birdfolk's Hollow Bones ability causes forced movement to move them **twice as far**, for example.
</Callout>
