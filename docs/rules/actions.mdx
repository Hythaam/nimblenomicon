# Actions

On your turn, heroes get **3 actions** to attack, move around the battlefield, cast spells, etc. All 3 actions recharge at the end of your turn.

## Core Actions

### Attack

Any spell or ability that can harm an enemy. Roll the die listed and deal that much damage. Rolling a 1 always misses.

**Exploding Critical Hits.** Rolling the max on a Primary Die is a critical hit. Roll the Primary Die again and add the result. Repeat on each maximum roll.

### Cast Spell

Requires one free hand (or a spellcasting focus), the ability to speak, and may require mana. A spell's mana cost equals its spell tier; cantrips cost 0 mana.

### Move

Move up to your speed (6 spaces by default). Can be split across other actions.

### Assess

Make a **DC 12 skill check** to ask a question, create an opening, or anticipate danger.

- **Ask a Question:** Ask about a weakness, ability, or immediate plans of enemies, the environment, or the story. The GM answers honestly.
- **Create an Opening:** Increase the next Primary Die Roll against a target by 1 this round.
- **Anticipate Danger:** Reduce all Primary Dice rolled against you by 1 this round.

The skill used must make sense given circumstances. You cannot Assess using the **same skill more than once** per encounter — foes adapt to your tactics.

### Bonus Action

Some class abilities and spells grant a **Bonus Action** — a faster, limited action that does not count against your 3 main actions.

### Free Action

Simple tasks such as opening an unlocked door, shouting a short phrase, dropping an item, or ending <RuleRef id="concentration" /> are **free** and do not cost an action. Limit: 1 free action per turn unless otherwise stated.

## Heroic Reactions

Reactions cost **1 action** and are performed when it is **not your turn**. Each reaction can be used no more than **1/round**; you begin your next turn with fewer actions.

### Defend

<RuleRef id="defend" /> — Reduce damage from any single attack by your **Armor** value. At the GM's discretion, some damage may not be avoidable (e.g., psychic damage, certain areas of effect).

### Interpose

<RuleRef id="interpose" /> — If a creature within **2 spaces** would be struck by an attack, you push them out of the way and become the new target. You move into their space; they move to an adjacent space of your choice.

<Callout type="note">
You can Interpose **and** Defend in the same moment as long as you have enough actions. You cannot use either reaction again until your next turn ends.
</Callout>

### Opportunity Attack

<RuleRef id="opportunity-attack" /> — Make a **melee attack with <RuleRef id="disadvantage" />** against an adjacent enemy as it **willingly moves away**. Only heroes can make opportunity attacks.

### Help

<RuleRef id="help" /> — Grant an ally <RuleRef id="advantage" /> on a roll if you can reasonably explain how you're helping (limit: one Help per roll). The GM may call for a skill check or grant advantage automatically, depending on the idea.

<Callout type="tip">
Help is one of the best ways to bring teamwork and creative role-playing into combat. A failed roll isn't always final — think quickly and see if you can turn it around.
</Callout>
