# Combat

A combat encounter begins when the GM tells the party to **"Roll Initiative!"** Each hero rolls 1d20 and adds their Initiative bonus (typically their DEX). A single digit means they start combat with 1 action. 2 digits, they start combat with 2. A result of 20+ (or a natural 20) means they get all 3 actions!

Regardless of what a hero rolls on Initiative, at the end of their turn they gain all 3 actions back.

## Turn Order

When combat begins, by default, the heroes go first. Monsters will typically act last, though some monsters may be fast enough to act sooner in the round.

## Turns, Rounds & Encounters

A **Turn** is when 1 individual hero or monster group acts. A typical turn takes roughly 6 seconds of "in-world" time. A **Round** is when all players and monsters have taken a turn acting. An **Encounter** is all of the rounds in a particular combat.

## Starting Combat & Turn Order

When combat begins, by default, the **heroes go first**. Whichever player is ready first takes the first turn (or whoever makes most narrative sense), with play proceeding around the table. Monsters act last, though some fast monsters may act sooner in a round. A monster group should act at the same time each round.

### Surprise

- **Heroes surprise enemies:** GM may grant advantage on Initiative, or heroes start with all 3 actions.
- **Heroes are surprised:** They may roll Initiative with disadvantage, or automatically start with 1 action. Monsters may go first.

> Merely being hidden or attacking first is not sufficient for Surprise — the enemy must be genuinely unaware of your presence.

### 1/Turn vs. 1/Round Abilities

- **1/Turn:** If you use it on your turn and find a way to use it on another turn (e.g., Opportunity Attack), you can.
- **1/Round:** Resets when your own turn begins.

### Acting Over Multiple Turns

For activities costing more than 1 action, actions can be spent over multiple turns as long as <RuleRef id="concentration" /> is maintained and no other actions or reactions are taken in the meantime (free actions are permitted).

## Heroic Reactions

Reactions cost **1 action** and are performed when it is **not your turn**. A hero can perform each reaction no more than **1/round** and will start their next turn with fewer actions.

### Defend

<RuleRef id="defend" /> — Reduce damage from any single attack by your **Armor**. At the GM's discretion, some damage may not be avoidable (e.g., psychic damage or certain areas of effect).

### Interpose

<RuleRef id="interpose" /> — If a creature within **2 spaces** would be struck by an attack, push them out of the way and become the new target. You enter their space and move them to an adjacent space of your choice.

<Callout type="note">
You can Interpose **and** Defend at the same time, as long as you have enough actions. However, you cannot use either reaction again until your next turn ends.
</Callout>

### Opportunity Attack

<RuleRef id="opportunity-attack" /> — Make a **melee attack with <RuleRef id="disadvantage" />** against an adjacent enemy as it **willingly moves away**. Only heroes can make opportunity attacks; monsters cannot.

### Help

<RuleRef id="help" /> — Grant an ally <RuleRef id="advantage" /> on a roll if you can reasonably explain to the GM how you help (limit: one Help per roll). The GM may call for a skill check or grant advantage automatically, depending on how creative or logical the help is.

<Callout type="tip">
**Teamwork!** Help is a fantastic way to bring creativity and role-playing into combat. Did your buddy fail? Think quickly — maybe you can turn that miss into a hit.
</Callout>

## Minions

Minions are low-threat monsters that are easy to kill individually but dangerous in numbers.

- **No HP to track.** Any damage kills a minion.
- **Easy attacks.** Each minion attacks with a single damage die, cannot crit, and misses on a roll of 1.
- **Simplified defense.** When multiple minions attack a single target, their damage is combined and counts as **a single attack** — heroes can <RuleRef id="defend" /> or <RuleRef id="interpose" /> against them all at once.

<Callout type="tip">
Move all minions attacking the same hero simultaneously. Roll all their dice at once, ignore any 1s, and add the results. Fast and easy!
</Callout>

## Conditions in Combat

Various effects can inflict <RuleRef id="conditions" label="conditions" /> on creatures. For example, a grappled creature gains the <RuleRef id="grappled" /> condition, reducing its speed to 0. Being struck by certain attacks might leave you <RuleRef id="prone" />.
