# Character Creation

Creating your hero is done in a few steps. Your class will have the largest impact on your other choices.

---

## Step 1: Choose Your Class

Your class defines your role, Key Stats, Hit Die size, and special abilities. Available classes include:

- **Berserker** — An unstoppable force of wrath and ruin
- **The Cheat** — Sneaky, backstabbing, dirty-fighting rogue
- **Commander** — A battlefield tactician, leader, and weapon master
- **Hunter** — Resourceful survivalist, bow master, and skilled tracker
- **Mage** — Wields and shapes the elements of fire, ice, and lightning
- **Oathsworn** — Faithful guardian, protector, and avenger of the weak
- **Shadowmancer** — Summons hordes of expendable minions
- **Shepherd** — Masters life and death; leads a faithful companion
- **Songweaver** — Inspiring presence, sharp wit, sharper tongue
- **Stormshifter** — Master of weather, beast, and nature
- **Zephyr** — A disciplined martial artist with swift hands and feet

---

## Step 2: Choose Your Ancestry & Background

Select one **Ancestry** (your lineage and heritage) and one **Background** (your life before adventuring). See the [Ancestries](ancestries) page for full details.

Optionally, decide your adventuring motivation and how you know the other heroes.

---

## Step 3: Fill In Your Character Sheet

### 1. Basic Details & Stats

Fill in your character's Name, Ancestry, Class, Level, Height, and Weight.

**Choose a Stat Array** and assign values to your four stats. Put the highest numbers in your Key Stats.

| Array | Values |
|-------|--------|
| **Standard** | +2, +2, +0, –1 |
| **Balanced** | +2, +1, +1, +0 |
| **Min–Max** | +3, +1, –1, –1 |

Mark your **advantaged (+) Save** and **disadvantaged (–) Save** on your sheet.

<Callout type="tip">
**Example (Min–Max):** The Cheat has DEX and INT as Key Stats. Put +3 in DEX, +1 in INT, and –1 in WIL and STR. Mark DEX saves as advantaged and WIL saves as disadvantaged.
</Callout>

### 2. Skill Points

At level 1:
- Mark your **stat bonuses** into each respective skill (e.g., +2 DEX → +2 Finesse and Stealth).
- Distribute **4 additional skill points** however you like (max +12 in any skill).

<Callout type="tip">
**Example:** With –1 INT, mark –1 in Arcana, Examination, and Lore. Then spend your 4 extra points: dump all into one skill, spread across four skills by +1 each, or anything in between.
</Callout>

### 3. Secondary Stats

| Stat | Default |
|------|---------|
| **Max HP** | From your class |
| **Hit Die size** | From your class |
| **Hit Die amount** | Equal to your level |
| **Initiative** | DEX |
| **Size** | Medium (unless ancestry says otherwise) |
| **Speed** | 6 |
| **Max Wounds** | 6 |
| **Inventory Slots** | 10 + STR |

### 4. Equipment & Money

Heroes start with either:
- The **equipment listed for their class and background**, or
- **50 gp** to purchase starting equipment

*Starting at a higher level? Multiply starting gold by your level.*

### 5. Languages

All heroes speak **Common** by default. Each point of INT grants **1 additional language**.

| Language | Typically Spoken By |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Common** | Most intelligent creatures |
| **Dwarvish** | Dwarves, Gnomes, and Giants |
| **Elvish** | Elves, Fey, and Sylvan creatures |
| **Goblin** | Goblins and Orcs |
| **Infernal** | Various Fiends and cultists |
| **Thieves' Cant** | Scoundrels and rogues |
| **Celestial** | Celestials and priests |
| **Draconic** | Dragons, Dragonborn, and Kobolds |
| **Primordial** | Elementals and Ancient Beings |
| **Deep Speak** | Underworld dwellers |

<Callout type="tip">
Speaking a creature's language can open doors that weapons alone cannot. Being heavily armed without the ability to communicate often leads to hostility.
</Callout>

### 6. Other Abilities

Note any special abilities from your class, ancestry, and background.

### 7. Inventory Slots

Each hero has **10 + STR** inventory slots.

| Slot Usage | Holds |
|------------|-------|
| 1 slot | One-handed weapon, shield, worn armor, stack of javelins, 500 gp, or 2 potions |
| 2 slots | Two-handed weapon, unworn armor, or similarly bulky items |
| 1 slot | Small related items grouped together (e.g., camping supplies) |

<Callout type="note">
In most games there's no need to track ammo. If you have a quiver, you have enough arrows — assume your hero collects fired arrows, crafts more, or buys them in town.
</Callout>

## Leveling Up

The GM awards a level when you complete an appropriately challenging quest. When you gain a level:

- **HP Increase:** Roll your Hit Die with advantage and increase your max HP by that amount.
- **More Endurance:** Your Hit Die maximum increases by 1 (typically equal to your level).
- **More Skilled:** Gain **1 skill point**. Additionally, move 1 point from one skill to another (as long as no skill goes negative), reflecting your evolving expertise.
- **Class Features:** Gain new class features — increased mana pool, new spells, subclass selection, etc.
- **Other Adjustments:** If base stats change, update skills, damage, Initiative, armor, mana, languages, and inventory slots as needed.

<Callout type="note">
The maximum bonus any skill can have is **+12**.
</Callout>
