# Stats & Skills

Heroes have **4 stats** that define their capabilities. Every hero has **2 Key Stats** (crucial to their class) and **2 Secondary Stats**. Stats range from –1 to +5 at maximum.

> **KEY.** When an ability or spell references "KEY," use one of your Key Stats. If a stat precedes a die roll, roll a number of dice equal to the stat — e.g., "WIL d8" with WIL 2 means roll 2d8.

## Stats

### Strength (STR)

Your raw physical power, endurance, and resilience. STR affects:
- STR weapon damage
- Resistance to Wounds
- HP recovery during Field Rests
- <RuleRef id="concentration" /> saves
- STR saving throws
- Carrying capacity (inventory slots = 10+STR)
- Grappling
- The **Might** skill

### Dexterity (DEX)

Your agility, reflexes, and precision. DEX affects:
- DEX weapon damage
- Initiative bonus
- DEX saving throws
- Grappling
- Armor (unarmored Armor = DEX)
- The **Stealth** and **Finesse** skills

### Intelligence (INT)

Your knowledge and reasoning. INT affects:
- Number of languages known (each +1 INT grants 1 additional language)
- Spellcasting
- Use of wands and spell scrolls
- INT saving throws
- The **Arcana**, **Examination**, and **Lore** skills

### Will (WIL)

Your force of personality, courage, and wisdom. WIL affects:
- Spellcasting
- WIL saving throws
- The **Insight**, **Influence**, **Naturecraft**, and **Perception** skills

---

## Stat Arrays

At character creation, choose one of these stat arrays and assign the values to your four stats (recommended: put the highest values in your Key Stats):

| Array | Values |
|-------|--------|
| **Standard** | +2, +2, +0, –1 |
| **Balanced** | +2, +1, +1, +0 |
| **Min–Max** | +3, +1, –1, –1 |

## Skills

Skills gauge how well your hero interacts with the world. Your starting skill bonus in each skill equals the governing stat. At level 1 you may distribute **4 additional skill points** freely. The maximum bonus a skill can ever have is **+12**.

| Skill | Stat | Used For |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Arcana** | INT | Identify magical effects, decipher arcane symbols, assess magical items; knowledge of Aberrations, Elementals, and Oozes |
| **Examination** | INT | Diagnose injuries, find clues, analyze traps and mechanical devices; knowledge of Constructs |
| **Finesse** | DEX | Pick locks, disarm traps, pilot vehicles, tinkering, sleight of hand, climbing mossy walls |
| **Influence** | WIL | Persuade, deceive, negotiate, build alliances, perform |
| **Insight** | WIL | Sense motives, detect lies, read emotions, retroactively "remember" useful preparations |
| **Lore** | INT | Recall historical events, cultural practices; knowledge of Celestials, Dragons, Fey, Fiends, Giants, Humanoids, Undead |
| **Might** | STR | Lift heavy objects, break through obstacles, climb, swim, jump, feats of strength |
| **Naturecraft** | WIL | Wilderness survival, navigation, tracking, handling animals; knowledge of Beasts, Monstrosities, Plants |
| **Perception** | WIL | Spot hidden objects, detect secret passages, sense being followed, notice non-obvious cues |
| **Stealth** | DEX | Hide, move silently, evade detection, slip past guards |

<Callout type="tip">
**Insight** can be used retroactively — e.g., if you forgot to buy rope, an Insight check might confirm your hero would have thought of it before leaving town.
</Callout>

## Skill Checks

When you want to affect the world (convince an NPC, spot a trap, pick a lock), the GM calls for a skill check. Roll **1d20 + skill bonus**. Meet or beat the DC to succeed.

- A roll of **1 always fails**, regardless of bonuses.
- A roll of **20 always succeeds**, regardless of DC.

| Difficulty | Example | DC |
|------------|---------|-----|
| Easy | Spotting a large Ogre behind a small bush | 8 |
| Medium | Finding a hidden door behind a bookcase | 12 |
| Challenging | Calming an injured Owlbear in a trap | 15 |
| Very Difficult | Reading the true intentions of a trained spy | 18 |
| Extremely Difficult | Disarming an ancient legendary trap | 20+ |

### Advantage & Disadvantage

Some situations grant <RuleRef id="advantage" /> or impose <RuleRef id="disadvantage" /> on a check. With advantage, roll an extra die and drop the lowest; with disadvantage, roll extra and drop the highest.

---

## Saves

When the world affects you, roll a **save** instead of a skill check. Roll **1d20 + the relevant stat**. A 1 always fails; a 20 always succeeds.

You may always **choose to fail** any save instead of rolling.

| Save | Stat | Resists |
|------|------|---------|
| **STR Save** | STR | Forced movement, restraint, poison, extreme temperatures |
| **DEX Save** | DEX | Explosions, area effects, icy terrain, anything requiring agility |
| **INT Save** | INT | Tricks, illusions, mental intrusions |
| **WIL Save** | WIL | Charm, fear, and effects targeting courage or personality |

### Hero Save Proficiency

Each hero has:
- **1 Advantaged Save (+)** — rolled with <RuleRef id="advantage" />
- **1 Disadvantaged Save (–)** — rolled with <RuleRef id="disadvantage" />
- **2 Neutral Saves**

Unless otherwise noted, the DC for effects a hero causes equals **10 + KEY**.
