# Grappling

Grappling is the act of grabbing, restraining, or wrestling a creature in melee.

## Requirements

To attempt a grapple, you must:
- Be within **Reach** of the target
- Have at least **1 arm free** (or some other means to grab)

## How to Grapple

Grappling is an **Attack action**. The target makes a **STR or DEX save** (their choice) against **DC 10 + your STR or DEX** (your choice, applied consistently).

### Outcome by Size

| Relative Size | Result |
|---------------|--------|
| Target is **your size or smaller** | Target gains the <RuleRef id="grappled" /> condition |
| Target is **larger than you** | You gain the <RuleRef id="riding" /> condition |

**<RuleRef id="grappled" />:** The grappled creature cannot move. Attacks against it have advantage.

**<RuleRef id="riding" />:** You move with the creature you are riding. Any attacks that miss you instead strike them.

## Escaping a Grapple

A grapple ends when any of the following occur:
- The grappled creature spends an **action** and succeeds on a **STR or DEX save** (same DC as the original grapple)
- The grappler is subject to **forced movement** (e.g., pushed away)
- The grappler is **incapacitated**

<Callout type="tip">
**Rushed Grapples:** If you grapple more than once on your turn, subsequent grapple attempts impose cumulative advantage on the enemy's save (instead of disadvantage on your roll, as with rushed attacks).
</Callout>

## Restrained

<RuleRef id="restrained" /> functions like Grappled — the creature cannot move and attacks against it have advantage — but it is caused by **objects** (chains, rope, roots, magical bindings) rather than another creature.

Because it is object-based, Restrained ignores size restrictions and can be ended through **any logical means**: picking a lock, cutting rope, burning vines, dispelling magic, etc.
