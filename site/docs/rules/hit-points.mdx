# Hit Points, Wounds & Recovery

## Hit Points (HP)

Hit Points represent your ability to endure damage. Damage reduces your HP, which can never go below 0. When you reach 0 HP, you gain **1 Wound** and the <RuleRef id="dying" /> condition.

---

## Dying

While <RuleRef id="dying" />, your actions are limited to **1 per turn** and <RuleRef id="concentration" /> is broken. You are also at risk of further serious harm:

- **Attacking or casting spells** causes **1 Wound** unless you succeed on a **DC 10 STR save**.
- **Taking damage while Dying** causes **2 Wounds**; a crit causes **3** instead.

You remain Dying until you regain at least 1 HP.

---

## Wounds

Wounds are serious injuries tracking how close you are to death. HP recovers quickly; Wounds linger.

- You gain 1 Wound whenever you drop to 0 HP.
- Wounds are typically recovered at a rate of **1 per Safe Rest**.
- At **6 Wounds**, you die (unless a class ability changes this number).

The <RuleRef id="wounded" /> condition applies to any hero who has at least 1 Wound.

<Callout type="note">
**Gritty Optional Rule:** GMs can reduce the maximum Wound threshold for a more lethal game — anywhere from 5 Wounds (slightly harder) down to 1–2 Wounds for extreme lethality. Each Wound can also impose a cumulative –1 penalty to rolls.
</Callout>

---

## Temporary HP

Some abilities and effects grant **temporary HP** (temp HP). Temp HP is reduced first when you take damage.

- Temp HP does **not** stack: if you would gain temp HP while you already have it, choose which amount to keep.
- Temp HP expires after a **Safe Rest**.

---

## Hit Dice

Hit Dice (HD) represent your ability to quickly recover from minor injuries. They are spent to regain HP during <RuleRef id="field-rest" />.

- Heroes start with a maximum of **1 Hit Die** at level 1.
- Your Hit Die maximum increases by **1 each time you level up** (equal to your level by default).
- Hit Dice are fully recovered during a **Safe Rest**.
- The size of your Hit Die is determined by your class.

### Spending Hit Dice

**During a Catch Breath (Field Rest):** Expend any number of Hit Dice one at a time. Roll each and add your STR. Regain that many HP.

**During Make Camp (Field Rest):** Take the maximum value for each Hit Die expended instead of rolling. Add STR as usual.

<Callout type="note">
If your STR is negative, subtract it from each HD you expend — your frail body doesn't recover as quickly as others.
</Callout>

---

## Concentration

Some activities — certain spells, sustained effects — require **Concentration** to maintain. A character can only concentrate on **one thing at a time**.

Concentration **breaks** when:
- The concentrating character is **critically hit** (unless they pass a **DC 10 STR save**)
- The character **drops to 0 HP**
- The character is <RuleRef id="incapacitated" />
- The character voluntarily ends it (free action)

<Callout type="tip">
When multi-action spells are cast over multiple turns, Concentration must be maintained throughout — and no other actions or reactions may be taken in the meantime (except free actions).
</Callout>
